LETS MAKE SOMETHING TO DRINK

Cold Drinks:

  • milkshake



    1. Combine 2 handfuls of ice cubes (approx. 10 ice cubes)
    2. 2 cups of milk
    3. 3/4 cup granulated sugar or 2 tablespoons honey
    4. and 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder in a blender
    5. Blend until smooth



  • mojito



    1. Lightly muddle the mint with the simple syrup in a shaker
    2. Add the rum, lime juice and ice
    3. give it a brief shake
    4. strain into a highball glass over fresh ice
    5. gently swizzle it all together
    6. Top with the club soda



  • Frappuccino




    1. cup of strong brewed coffee or 2 shots of espresso
    2. cup of milk (dairy or non-dairy)
    3. tablespoons of sugar or your preferred sweetener
    4. cup of ice cubes
    5. Optional: flavored syrups, chocolate or caramel sauce, whipped cream, and toppings



Hot drinks:

  • cofee




    1. In a cup, take 1.5 teaspoons of instant coffee
    2. 1.5 tablespoons sugar (or add as per taste)
    3. Add 3 tablespoons hot boiling water
    4. First mix the coffee and sugar with water
    5. Then begin to stir briskly and beat coffee for 3 to 4 minutes



  • hot chocolate




    1. Place the milk of your choice in a saucepan over medium-low heat.
    2. Whisk in cocoa powder and sugar, and heat until warm
    3. Once the milk is warm, add chocolate chips, whisking until they melt into the milk.
    4. Add a splash of vanilla extract
    5. Serve immediately, topped with your favorite garnishes: marshmallows, whipped cream, chopped chocolate, crushed candy canes or more



  • turkish tea




    1. Turkish black tea leaves, preferably from the Black Sea region, celebrated for their robust flavor
    2. Filtered or bottled water, as the water quality is paramount to the tea's final taste
    3. For every 100 ml of water, use about 1 full teaspoon (4 gram) of tea. Adjust this ratio based on your taste preferences
    4. Optional: Enhance your tea with additives like Earl Grey, clove, or a cinnamon stick. Sugar cubes and lemon slices are also popular additions
